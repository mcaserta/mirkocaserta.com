I was reading this book by Paolo Nori in which at some point he says: "a madman
is a useless masterpiece, and there is nothing more to say". So says Paolo Nori.
It has to be said that Paolo Nori has a taste for the one-liner, and there is
nothing wrong with that. A one-liner is necessarily a simplification and I think
there is value in simplification. But there is also value in depth and
complexity. Synthesis and analysis are two sides of the same coin. But I did not
want to talk about this, I wanted to talk about the fact that the madman is
extremely useful. A madman shows you alternative realities, he reveals to you
possibilities so soaked in fantasy that a sane person cannot only see them, but
cannot even conceive of them. The madman lives reality with detachment and this
allows him a freedom of vision that the presumed sane lack. I do not know about
you but I always feel envy when I recognise a genuine madman because I would
like to be in his place, at least for a while. Who does not like to play the
fool to avoid going to war? Our war is going along with the gears of society and
the madman reminds us that we live in a construction and that the construction
can be taken apart and put back together, or destroyed and left in dust. That is
why I find that the madman is a masterpiece not just useful but necessary. Have
more sympathy for madmen and less for the serious doom-mongers with a stick up
their arse, because you can be sure you have more fun and live better with the
former. And in any case this is not meant as a criticism of Paolo Nori who, to
be clear, I still find likeable, if only because he is obsessed with the
Russians.
